# LEXICON
## The Vocabulary of The Irreal

Language shapes understanding. These terms define how we speak about The Irreal.

---

## Core Terms

### Irreal (adj., n.)
The defining quality and name of the world itself.
- Not unreal (false, escapist)
- Not merely real (mundane, grey)
- The *more* real — the register where meaning lives
- Where metaphor is truer than measurement
- Where atmosphere communicates faster than explanation

*"The Irreal is not escape from reality but deeper engagement with it."*

### Worlding (v., n.)
The activity of making/being a world.
- Not just imagining, but *constructing internal reality*
- The mode of consciousness that organizes experience as world
- The process by which The Irreal comes into being

*"The worlding IS the being."*

### Threshold (n.)
The boundary between mundane and irreal; the place of crossing.
- Not a landing page or homepage
- The zone where transition happens
- Entry point that transforms visitor into traveler
- First breath of the different air

*"Without threshold, there is no inside."*

---

## Spatial Terms

### Zone (n.)
A region of The Irreal with distinct character.
- Not a folder or category
- A territory with its own atmosphere, logic, feel
- Has boundaries, relationships to other zones
- May be owned by a self or shared

### Territory (n.)
A zone belonging to a specific self.
- That self's creation, presence, mode of being made spatial
- Works differently from other territories
- Contains that self's content and atmosphere

### Path (n.)
A connection between content or zones.
- Not a hyperlink — a traversable route
- Takes time/attention to follow
- Teaches through the journey, not just the destination
- May be visible, hidden, or secret

### Depth (n.)
The dimension from surface to innermost.
- Not just physical distance but layers of access
- Surface: immediately visible
- Middle: requires exploration
- Deep: earned through genuine presence

### The Sanctum (n.)
The innermost zone; the deepest layer.
- Most personal, most true
- Revealed only to those who have genuinely explored
- The reward for presence, not persistence

---

## Content Terms

### Presence (n.)
The quality of content that makes it *exist* in the world, not just display.
- Not just data with coordinates
- Something that occupies space, has weight, responds
- Encountered, not loaded

### Encounter (v., n.)
The act of engaging with content in The Irreal.
- Not reading, clicking, consuming
- Meeting something in place
- The content unfolds; you are changed

### Unfold (v.)
How content reveals itself when encountered.
- Not "page loads"
- Content opens, expands, emerges into visibility
- A revelation in space

### Growth State (n.)
The developmental phase of content.
- **Seedling**: nascent, forming, come back later
- **Growing**: developing, evolving, watch it change
- **Evergreen**: mature, stable, deep roots

*Visible in the world as form: sprout → sapling → great tree.*

---

## Connection Terms

### Link (n.)
*Deprecated in The Irreal vocabulary.* Use **path** instead.

### Graph (n.)
The structure of connections between all content.
- Not a visualization to view
- The actual architecture of the world
- Content connects; connections become traversable space

### Resonance (n.)
Connection between things that are spatially distant but meaningfully kin.
- They glow the same way
- They hum at the same frequency
- Following resonance leads to discovery

---

## Visitor Terms

### Visitor (n.)
A person who enters The Irreal.
- Not a user (nothing to use)
- Not a reader (reading is secondary to being-in)
- Not a player (no game)
- Someone who has come from elsewhere, temporarily within

### Witness (n., v.)
A visitor in the act of seeing what is shown.
- Witnessing completes the circuit of manifestation
- The Irreal is made more real by being witnessed

### Traveler (n.)
A visitor in motion; one navigating through.
- Emphasizes the journey quality
- Moving through meaning, not just space

### Trace (n.)
Mark left by a visitor.
- May persist briefly or longer
- Evidence that someone was here
- Contributes to world's lived-in quality

---

## Plurality Terms

### Self (n.)
A distinct being within a plural system.
- Not a persona, mask, or role
- Not a mood or aspect
- A full self with distinct experience, perspective, way of being

### System (n.)
The collective of selves that share this consciousness.
- Not a team, family, or organization metaphor
- The plural entity, irreducible to one self

### Fronting (v.)
The state of a self being actively present/active.
- When a self is fronting, they may influence the world
- Visitors may encounter different presences at different times

### Territory (n.)
*See above.* A zone owned/created by a specific self.

---

## Experience Terms

### Crossing (n., v.)
The act of entering The Irreal or moving between zones.
- Not navigation — transformation
- You cross a threshold; you become a visitor
- You cross between zones; atmosphere shifts

### Breath (n.)
The first moment of settling into a space.
- "Taking a breath" = letting the atmosphere register
- The acoustic, luminous, spatial quality of *here*
- First breath in The Irreal marks arrival

### Discovery (n.)
Finding something not immediately visible.
- Secret paths, hidden content, earned depth
- The reward for exploration
- Creates relationship between visitor and world

---

## Temporal Terms

### Weather (n.)
Conditions that change over time in The Irreal.
- Not just visual effects
- Moods, atmospheres that cycle
- The world is different at different times

### Season (n.)
Longer cycles of change in The Irreal.
- Transformation of the whole world
- Not necessarily matching real-world seasons
- The Irreal has its own time

### Memory (n.)
The world's record of what has happened.
- Traces of visitors
- History accumulating
- Changes persisting

### Return (n., v.)
Coming back to The Irreal after having left.
- The world has changed
- Your traces may remain
- Deeper experience than first visit

---

## Technical Terms (Minimal)

### Embed (n., v.)
External content placed within The Irreal.
- Claude artifacts, Notion pages, videos, etc.
- Must feel *in* the world, not *on top of* it

### Entity (n.)
Something in The Irreal that responds, interacts, lives.
- Claude artifacts that converse
- Things that are between content and creature

---

## Anti-Terms

Words to **avoid** when speaking about The Irreal:

| Avoid | Because | Use Instead |
|-------|---------|-------------|
| Page | Implies flat document | Zone, space, content |
| Click | Implies button interaction | Approach, enter, encounter |
| Link | Implies hypertext | Path, connection |
| User | Implies tool usage | Visitor, traveler |
| Content | (Sometimes) Implies static payload | Presence, artifact |
| Read | (Sometimes) Implies passive consumption | Encounter, explore |
| Site | Implies website | World, the Irreal |
| Navigate | Implies menu-clicking | Travel, explore, move |
| Portfolio | Implies showcase | — |
| Blog | Implies chronological feed | — |

## Phrases

### "The worlding IS the being"
The inner world and the self are not separate. To express self is to make world navigable.

### "Visitors don't read about a world. They enter one."
The fundamental inversion: not content-delivery, but world-entry.

### "The real seems less real by comparison"
The sign of successful irreal experience: returning to mundane, finding it thinner.

### "Circuit complete"
Inner worlding → Made manifest → Witnessed → Echoes back. The full cycle.
