# PHENOMENOLOGY
## The Felt Experience of The Irreal

This document describes not features but *feelings*. It is the experiential specification that all technical decisions must serve. If an implementation is technically correct but phenomenologically wrong, it fails.

---

## I. The Arrival

### Before Entry
You've clicked a link. Or typed a URL. The mundane gestures of web navigation. You expect: a page loads, content appears, you scroll, you click, you leave.

### The Crossing
But instead: **something happens**.

Not a splash screen. Not a loading bar. Not "press any key to continue."

A *transition*. You feel:
- The browser context fading (not literally, but experientially)
- Something gathering, preparing
- A threshold you're about to cross
- The distinct sensation: "I am about to be *somewhere*"

### The First Breath
And then you're in.

The first sensory impressions:
- **Sound** arrives before or with sight — not music yet, but *air*. The acoustic signature of a space.
- **Light** has quality — not UI brightness, but *illumination*. Golden hour, or twilight, or the sourceless glow of dreaming.
- **Scale** — you understand you are small within something larger. Not claustrophobic, not agoraphobic. Held.

You take a breath. The breath feels like it belongs here. **You have arrived.**

---

## II. The Orientation

### Not a Tutorial
You are not told what to do. There are no tooltips, no "click here to begin," no onboarding flow.

Instead:
- You find yourself somewhere. You look around.
- Some directions feel more interesting than others. Curiosity has *direction* here.
- You realize: you can move. Not because you were told, but because spaces invite movement.

### Environmental Legibility
The world teaches you itself by being itself:
- Things that can be interacted with feel *present*, not just visible
- Paths don't need signs; they invite by being paths
- Distances feel meaningful — going *there* is a commitment
- The grammar of the space is learnable through inhabitation

### First Choice
Early — very early — you encounter your first choice. Not a menu. A fork in attention.

"I could go *there*... or I could go *there*..."

This moment is critical. You're not being directed. You're **choosing to explore**. Agency has been transferred.

## III. The Movement

### Not WASD
The kinesthetic experience of moving through The Irreal should not feel like a video game. Not the FPS trot, not the platformer hop.

Instead:
- **Gliding** — momentum has softness, starts and stops have curves
- **Drawn toward** — you don't push through space, you're pulled by interest
- **Intentional drift** — movement expresses desire, not just direction
- **Presence in motion** — you remain aware of yourself moving, not just destination approaching

### The Feel of Distance
Going somewhere should *feel* like going somewhere:
- Not instant teleportation (usually)
- Not tedious slogging
- The journey is part of the meaning
- Arrival is earned through attention, not time

### Different Movements in Different Zones
Not all of The Irreal moves the same:
- Some zones may feel more gravity-bound, deliberate
- Some may feel floatier, more dreamlike
- Some may compress distance (things come to you)
- Some may expand distance (depth requires commitment)

## IV. The Encounter

### Approaching Content
You see something in the distance. It glows, or pulses, or has presence. You move toward it.

As you approach:
- It responds to your approach (subtly — not performing for you, but acknowledging)
- Detail resolves (not loading — *revealing*)
- The space around it becomes its context
- You feel: "I am about to encounter something"

### The Unfold
You interact (click, approach threshold, attend). The content doesn't "load." It **unfolds**.

- Not a page appearing. A *revelation in space*.
- The content has context — it lives *here*, this location matters
- Reading happens *in place*, not in displaced modal
- You can still feel the world around you while engaging

### After the Encounter
You finish. You step back. The content folds but doesn't vanish — it remains a presence, a thing you've now encountered.

You've changed. Subtly. You know something now. You've been somewhere.

**The world remembers this. You remember this. The visit matters.**

---

## V. The Connection

### Paths Between
Content is connected to other content. These connections are not hyperlinks. They are **paths**.

When you've encountered something, you notice:
- Threads of light, or visible paths, or open doorways leading *elsewhere*
- These elsewhere-places are related — you feel the relationship before you understand it
- Following a connection is a *journey*, not a click
- The path teaches you something the destination can't

### The Felt Relationship
Being in The Irreal over time, you develop a felt sense:
- "Things over *there* are related to things over *here*"
- "When I go deeper in this direction, the content becomes..."
- "These two distant things glow the same way — they resonate"

This is knowledge through habitation. Map-knowledge. Bodily understanding of the world's topology.

## VI. The Zones

### Zone Transitions
Moving between major zones is not seamless. It's a **crossing**.

You feel:
- The atmosphere shifting before you've fully arrived
- Color, sound, light — all changing
- A different *mode of being* settling over you
- Recognition: "I am somewhere different now"

### Each Zone Has Character
Not "different themes" but **different realities**:

**The Threshold**
- First breath quality
- Multiple possibilities visible
- Welcoming but not directing
- Anticipation, not information

**The Workshop** (or its true name)
- Energy of creation, of mess, of becoming
- Things in process, unfinished, raw
- Energetic, maybe chaotic
- The warmth of work

**The Grove** (or its true name)
- Growth, patience, tending
- Things finding their form
- Quieter, more spacious
- The feel of gardens

**The Gallery** (or its true name)
- Completion, presentation
- Things ready to be witnessed
- More formal, more refined
- The hush of museums

**The Deep** (or its true name)
- Descent, interiority
- Core mythology, source material
- Darker, denser
- The pressure of depth

**The Selves** (or its true name)
- Multiplicity, difference
- Each territory genuinely different
- Disorientation when crossing
- The strangeness of plurality

**The Sanctum** (or its true name)
- Innermost, most true
- Earned arrival
- Intimacy
- The reward for genuine exploration

## VII. The Secrets

### Hidden Things
Not everything is visible. The Irreal rewards exploration with **discovery**.

Types of secrets:
- **Spatially hidden** — off the main paths, in corners, behind things
- **Perceptually hidden** — visible only to those who *attend* properly
- **Temporally hidden** — present only at certain times, certain conditions
- **Relationally hidden** — revealed only after encountering other things

### The Feel of Discovery
Finding something hidden should feel like:
- "Wait, what's this?"
- A small thrill, a private gift
- The world rewarding your attention
- A secret between you and The Irreal

### Earning Depth
The deepest content is not just hidden. It is **earned**.

You don't earn through:
- Achievements, points, gamification
- Time spent (grinding)
- Puzzles solved (arbitrary gates)

You earn through:
- Genuine attention (engaging deeply, not skimming)
- Return (coming back, noticing change)
- Following connections (letting interest lead)
- Presence (being *in* it, not passing through)

---

## VIII. The Living World

### Things Change
When you return to The Irreal, it should not be identical:
- Seedlings have grown (or faded, if untended)
- Light is different (time of day, season)
- Your traces from before are subtly present
- New things have appeared; some things have moved

### The Sense of Time
The Irreal has its own time:
- Not real-world clock time (though that might influence it)
- Cycles that are internal to the world
- History that accumulates
- A future that approaches

### Aliveness Without Busyness
"Alive" does not mean:
- Constant motion, noise, stimulation
- NPCs walking around, things happening at you
- Notifications, updates, feeds

"Alive" means:
- The sense that this place exists whether you're here or not
- Things grow, change, breathe at their own pace
- Memory — the world knows what happened
- Presence — someone *lives* here

---

## IX. The Departure

### Leaving
At some point, you decide to leave. Or you must leave. Or you drift away.

How leaving should feel:
- Not abrupt (not just closing tab, though that's technically what happens)
- A sense of *withdrawal*, not just stopping
- The world remains behind — you're leaving, not ending it
- The thought: "I'll come back"

### After
When you've left The Irreal and returned to mundane browsing:
- The memory should persist — not as content recalled but as *place* visited
- You should be able to tell someone: "I was in this world"
- The mundane should feel slightly thinner for a moment

This is the sign of successful irreal experience: **the real seems less real by comparison**.

---

## X. Phenomenological Tests

When building, these are the questions:

**Threshold:**
Does entry feel like *crossing into*?

**Presence:**
Does the space feel *inhabited*?

**Movement:**
Does navigation feel like *travel*, not clicking?

**Encounter:**
Does content feel *encountered*, not loaded?

**Connection:**
Do links feel like *paths*, not hyperlinks?

**Zone:**
Do transitions feel like *arriving somewhere different*?

**Life:**
Does returning feel like *things happened while you were gone*?

**Departure:**
Does leaving feel like *leaving a place*?

If any answer is no, the implementation is wrong — regardless of technical correctness.
